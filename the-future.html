The Future

Artemis Missions

The Artemis missions are NASA's attempt at bringing humanity back to the moon. They're named after Artemis, the greek goddess of the moon, twin sister of Apollo. It pays homage to the Apollo missions of the past while still opening a new chapter of space exploration. Artemis will land the first woman and person of colour on the moon, and work to build a long term home there.

SpaceX Starship

The SpaceX starship is a spacecraft designed to be able to launch, land, and fly again, making space travel cheaper and more sustainable. Much like modern airplanes, these spacecraft will be able to be quickly refurbished and relaunched rather than having to be entirely discarded. It aims to achieve this with a fully reuable booster and Starship spacecraft that can carry crew and cargo. These rockets could revolutionise space exploration and usher in a new space age of unprecedented scale.

Human Mars Mission

Human Mars missions aim to send astronauts to the Red Planet for the first time, marking a giant leap in space exploration. These missions will involve long-duration spaceflight, advanced life support systems, and habitats that can protect crews from harsh conditions. NASA's Artemis program and SpaceX's Starship are helping pave the way. The goal is to explore Mars's surface, search for signs of life, and lay the groundwork for a long-term human presence on another world.

Asteroid Mining

Asteroid mining is the idea of extracting valuable resources like metals, water, and minerals from asteroids in space. These materials could support space industries, provide fuel for missions, or even supply rare metals for use on Earth. Companies and space agencies are exploring technologies for locating, capturing, and processing asteroids. Mining in space could reduce the need to launch heavy materials from Earth and help build a sustainable space economy, supporting future exploration of the Moon, Mars, and beyond.
